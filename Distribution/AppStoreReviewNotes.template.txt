Slaptop is a free, open-source Utilities app licensed under the MIT License. It
translates intentional taps on a MacBook display into the user's configured
Mission Control and Spaces keyboard shortcuts. Users can use the default left,
right, and top tap actions or configure custom gesture patterns and shortcut
mappings.

The Mac App Store build is sandboxed, contains no updater or privileged helper,
and collects or transmits no user data. It requests the
com.apple.security.temporary-exception.iokit-user-client-class entitlement for
IOHIDLibUserClient solely for read-only accelerometer and gyroscope input from
the built-in AppleSPU HID device. The related missing-functionality report is
Feedback Assistant case @APP_STORE_FEEDBACK_ID@. The complete entitlement
justification is entered in App Sandbox Usage Information.

To test, run Slaptop on an Apple Silicon MacBook, complete calibration, and tap
the left or right side of the display to move between Spaces, or tap the top to
open Mission Control. The Settings window also provides Test buttons and custom
pattern controls. No account or network connection is required.
